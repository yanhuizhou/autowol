Name - Arbitrary device identifier. Doesn't need to be the same as the name of the real device.
Mac - Mac address of the network card of the device to wake.
Broadcast IP/FQDN - Network broadcast IP (most commonly 192.168.0.255 and 192.168.1.255) or FQDN (fully qualified domain name - something like whatever.dyndns.com). This is an IP address or public FQDN used by your router to broadcast network packets.
Port - Port on which your device is listening for Wake-on-Lan packets (most commonly 9 and 7). Do not change unless you know what you're doing.
Auto-Wake - If enabled, AutoWOL will send WOL packets to the device as soon as your android device gets connected to the Wi-Fi.
Wi-Fi SSID - Wi-Fi network SSID is your wireless access point (router) name. WOL packets will be automatically sent to the device only when connected to this AP.
Quiet hours - Period during which automatic Wake-on-Lan will be suppressed. We don't want the app to turn the device on during the night on random Wi-Fi disconnects, do we? :)
Idle time - Period after last Wi-Fi disconnect during which automatic Wake-on-Lan will be suppresed. This can be used to suppress sending WOL packets during the day on random Wi-Fi disconnects (will not ignore Quiet hours restrictions).
